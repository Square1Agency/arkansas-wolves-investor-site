03 / Deal

Three ways
to be part of this.

Professional soccer ownership is a passion play. It is a legacy play. It is a community-asset play. It is not — and we are not pretending it is — a vehicle for outsized financial returns in years one through three. Here is how the deal is structured, honestly.

The Three Paths

Pick the role that fits.

Path 1

Sole Owner

65–100% of the club. Full operational control, full upside on franchise value appreciation over a decade, and the full reputational and legacy benefit of building Arkansas' first pro soccer club. League floor: $10M net worth excluding primary residence.

For: 1 deeply committed principal
Path 2

Lead Investor in a Group

35%+ ownership inside a multi-owner group. The lead partner sets direction and meets the league's net-worth requirement; co-owners diversify the risk and bring complementary businesses to the table.

For: 1 anchor + 4–9 partners
Path 3

Community Shareholder

10–20% of the club reserved for community ownership, modeled on Chattanooga FC and the Green Bay Packers. Smaller share size, broader local base, builds an army of supporters who literally own a piece of the club.

For: 100–500 local investors
League Requirements

USL One requires the lead owner (or the lead member of an ownership group, holding 35%+) to have a net worth of $10M minimum, excluding primary residence. In practice, the league has learned that the $10M floor is too low — clubs running on that margin tend to burn out by year three.

Our target is $20M+ on the lead position. That's not a barrier; it's a safety net. Soccer ownership is a passion play, but the operating runway has to be there.

The Honest Math

MLS has been around 30 years. Exactly one team has finished a season in the black. Minor league soccer is the same picture, smaller. Years one through three of operations are loss-years for almost every club, regardless of how well it's run.

What appreciates is the franchise itself. An MLS franchise that cost $1M in 1996 is worth $700M today. The capital return story exists; it just operates on a 10-to-20-year horizon, not a 3-to-5-year one.

If your investment thesis is "double my money in five years," we will respectfully point you to commercial real estate. If it is "build something that outlives me, in a city I care about, while my primary business stays my primary business" — we should talk.

The Adjacency Play

Your business benefits
from being in the room.

The Phoenix Rising ownership group is intentionally built around adjacent businesses. The result: most of the club's largest line-item costs are absorbed by ownership-affiliated businesses, often at lower internal pricing.

Where Revenue Comes From

Eight to ten streams.
All routed through the venue.

Ticketing. Parking. Concessions. Merchandise. Jersey sponsorships. Player contract sales. Broadcast revenue. Plus every non-soccer event the venue can host — concerts, graduations, semi-pro football, rugby, fitness events, ice skating, holiday markets. Together they are the basis of a sustainable operation. Apart, none of them work.
