The Pathway

Eight years old to the senior team.

The Wolves Academy is already the largest youth development system in Arkansas. Wolves PDA — the state's first Player Development Academy — has trained hundreds of elite players. The first professional player signing happened in 2024. The path from local kid to professional soccer player, on Wolves badges the whole way, is operational. The venue is what closes the loop.

The Academy as Engine

In well-run lower-division clubs, the youth academy is not a cost center — it's the financial backbone. Across the country, clubs like Phoenix Rising and many others have built academies that fully sustain their professional team's operations. Parents pay for elite training; that revenue cross-subsidizes the senior side and gives the pro team a stable financial floor it can plan against.

600+ kids in our academy today, growing year-over-year. With a venue and a complex around it, that number scales 2–3x in five years and the economics of the senior team change permanently.

The Fan Experience

The CMO mandate is simple:
own the fan experience.

MLS spent five years and a lot of money figuring out their demographic: 18–35, college-educated, disposable income. That's the same demographic every minor league soccer club is fighting for. We've already got them watching us on social. The next move is converting them into season-pass-holders by making the in-venue experience the best three hours they have all month.
